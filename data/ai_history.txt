Artificial Intelligence (AI) has a long and storied history, starting from ancient myths of artificial beings endowed with consciousness. The modern field of AI was officially born at a workshop at Dartmouth College in 1956. Key figures included John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Allen Newell, and Herbert Simon. Since then, AI has experienced several "AI winters" followed by periods of rapid progress, particularly with the advent of deep learning in the 2010s.
